Black Friday Plea Bargain

May 25, 2011 - 4:52 PM EDT
By Noah Stephens-Davidowitz

On May 23rd, Subject: Poker reported that American citizen Bradley Franzen had plead guilty to various charges related to the Black Friday indictments. We now have obtained the full plea agreement between Franzen and the United States Attorney for the Southern District of New York.

The plea bargain requires Franzen to “truthfully and completely disclose all information with respect to the activities of himself and others concerning all matters about which this Office [the US Attorney for the SDNY] inquires of him,” as well as to provide all requested documents and to testify in any future trials when requested. In exchange, the US Attorney will formally request that Franzen be sentenced leniently.

In a press release from the US Attorney for SDNY, Franzen is described as an intermediary who operated a Costa Rica-based business that linked online gambling sites1 with payment processors in the US, many of whom allegedly defrauded banks in order to move money for the sites. This position would likely have provided him with intimate knowledge of the US payment operations of the companies involved in the April 15th indictment and likely those of other online gambling companies as well.

Franzen faces up to thirty years in prison and up to $1 million in fines for charges of conspiracy to commit bank fraud, accepting funds in connection with unlawful internet gambling2, and conspiracy to commit money laundering.

Footnotes
  1. This includes all three sites involved in the April 15th indictment. ↩
  2. The press release clarifies that this is illegal because of the 2006 Unlawful Internet Gambling Enforcement Act. ↩
