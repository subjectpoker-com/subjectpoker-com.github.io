Merge Refusing New Players from Some States

May 24, 2011 - 8:10 PM EDT
By Noah Stephens-Davidowitz

We continue to see possible fallout from the new indictments handed down yesterday as the Merge Poker Network has decided to stop accepting new customers from Washington D.C., Louisiana, Missouri, and Maryland. They have also reiterated their continued desire to not accept new customers from the state of New York.

Some of these states should come as no surprise, as many of them have been hostile towards online gambling. In particular, Maryland participated in handing down indictments to three people and two companies yesterday. Ten associated domains were also seized, including Bookmaker.com and Doylesroom.com. Oddly absent from the list is the state of Washington, which passed a stringent law against online gambling in 2006 and whose citizens were barred from Poker Stars and Full Tilt after the state Supreme Court upheld the law in 2010. Sources close to Merge tell us that, as of right now, Washington is not on the list of barred states.

The Merge Network is still allowing users who have had accounts in the past from these states to continue playing for real money. This is similar to the recent actions of Hero Poker, which stopped accepting all US customers but allowed already registered US players to continue playing for real money. It is not clear how the US government will interpret such actions legally as opposed to the actions of sites like Victory Poker, which has completely withdrawn from the US market.

According to PokerScout.com Merge is the largest remaining network serving US players, with roughly twice as much traffic as its nearest competitor, Bodog.
