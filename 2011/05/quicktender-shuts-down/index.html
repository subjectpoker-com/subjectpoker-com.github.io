QuickTender Shuts Down

May 25, 2011 - 4:06 PM EDT
By Noah Stephens-Davidowitz

The e-wallet QuickTender shut its doors today after weeks of uncertainty.

QuickTender, which also operates under the name UseMyWallet, informed its customers this morning by e-mail:

Dear _____

Since our last email communication where we advised any funds in your QuickTender account could still be used to purchase at QuickTender accepting sites we had been working hard to reinstate our withdrawal facility and ensuring your funds where safe and secure.

However, further developments specifically relating to our main bank processing company have resulted in our accounts being frozen subject to a seizure order giving us no ability to make payments in any currency.

In light of these developments we have had no option but to discontinue the Quicktender service with immediate effect and for the foreseeable future.

At this time we are unable to confirm when or if we could make payments of remaining balances.

We will keep you advised of future relevant developments.

QuickTender Support

QuickTender customers had been reporting troubles withdrawing from the e-wallet for weeks. More recently, the company had reportedly responded to customer inquiries with a variety of e-mails expressing its apparent confusion about and lack of control over the situation with player funds and blaming vague problems with banking partners. On May 18th, the company released a statement on Casinomeister private forums, stating in part:

It emerges that the money has been accumulated in our bank’s correspondent banking partners accounts in the US, and has been frozen. We suspect it is possible that these funds may be subject to a seizure order by the US authorities. We would stress that we do not have bank accounts in the US of our own, and that the funds have left our account Europe.

It is not immediately clear how this is related to the April 15th or May 23rd indictments. Although it has been widely reported otherwise, neither QuickTender nor UseMyWallet are mentioned in any of the public legal documents related to the indictments. It is possible that QuickTender is a subsidiary of one of the companies affected by the indictments or that the seizure of the e-wallets accounts are unrelated to the 4/15 and 5/23 indictments. Subject: Poker is working to learn more about this issue, and we will report what we find as soon as we know.
