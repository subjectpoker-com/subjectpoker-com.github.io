Merge Network Temporarily Blocks US Sign-Ups

June 1, 2011 - 11:17 PM EDT
By Noah Stephens-Davidowitz

The Merge Gaming Network plans to temporarily stop accepting new US players.

A source close to Merge who requested anonymity has confirmed this story to Subject: Poker, corroborating the reports of other news outlets. We were unable to find a clear timeline, but our source described the move as “very temporary.”1 The decision will not affect current US players or players outside of the US.

Both our source and other reports claim that this was done to give the Merge Network more time to handle the large influx of US players brought on by the April 15th indictment. The events of Black Friday caused the then three largest US-facing poker sites, Poker Stars, Full Tilt Poker, and the Cereus Network to pull out of the American market, making Merge by far the largest remaining US-facing network. Indeed, since the events of Black Friday, the Merge Network has grown by 76% according to PokerScout.com.2

This news comes after the Merge Network stopped US player sign-ups from four states on May 24th, likely in response to the May 23rd indictments. In addition, Merge skins Hero Poker, Sportsbook.com, and PlayersOnly have all stopped accepting new US players since Black Friday.

With Merge out of the picture for new US players for the immediate future, unsigned US players are left with Bodog, the Cake Network, the Everleaf Gaming Network, and the Yatahay Poker Network.3 Yatahay was the subject of the May 23rd indictments, but unlike the sites involved in the April 15th indictment, many Yatahay skins have chosen to continue their operations.

Edited on 6/2/2011 1:47 AM EST: Changed the estimated 50% increase in Merge Network’s traffic to the more exact numbers provided by PokerScout.com.

Footnotes
  1. We are working to learn an exact timeline. ↩
  2. PokerScout.com reported to Subject: Poker that Merge’s seven-day average cash-game traffic was 929 on April 14th and is now 1631. ↩
  3. List taken from PokerScout.com. There are other poker sites that take US players, but they have little or no traffic. ↩
